Data and Variables
Click on the title to get the data!

  • Income Index Gross national income (GNI) per capita (Purchasing power parity $) that can measure the extent to which the distribution of income among individuals. 0 represents perfect equality, while an index of 1 implies perfect inequality.

  • Urban Population People living in urban areas as defined by national statistical offices. It is calculated using World Bank population estimates and urban ratios from the United Nations World Urbanization Prospects.

  • Corruption Perception Score A score of the perceived levels of public sector corruption determined by expert assessments and opinion surveys on a scale from 100 (very clean) to 0 (highly corrupt).

  • Education Index A combination of average adult Years of Schooling and Expected Years of Schooling for children, each receiving 50% weighting. The value of it is between 0(Low education)~ 1 (high education).

  • International Arrivals The number of tourists who travel to a country other than that in which they have their usual residence, but outside their usual environment, for a period not exceeding 12 months and whose main purpose in visiting is other than an activity remunerated from within the country visited.


  • *The natural logarithms transformations of variables are used in the model.


Methodology

Steps

Step 1 Variable Test

The correlation coefficient between ln Income Index and ln Education index is very high (0.8219, close to 1).

But the Variance Inflation Factor (VIF) of all variables are less than 10, there is no high collinearity between variables.

Step 2 Unit Root Test

All variables pass the Unit Root Test at the level form.

The panel data is stationary.

Therefore, it is no necessary to conduct the Cointegration Test.



FT- F-test
PM- Pooled Regression Model
FM - Fixed Effective Model
RM- Random Effective Model
ST- Serial Correlation Test
HT- Heteroskedasticity Test
CT- Cross-sectional Dependence Test
CHT- Classical Hausman Test

Step 3 Model Selection

The result of F-test shows the Fixed Effect Model is a good choice. However, the panel data has the Serial Correlation, Heteroskedasticity, Cross-sectional Dependence. Hence, the Modified Hausman Test are used to determine which the Fixed Effects Model or the Random Effects model is more appropriate for the data. The Modified Hausman Test suggests that the Fixed Effect Model is more suitable.


Step 4 Estimation

Due to the existence of Serial Correlation, Heteroskedasticity, Cross-sectional Dependence, we use Fixed Effect Model with Robust Standard Error to improve the accuracy of its estimation results.


What Factors Drive the Revenue of Luxury Industry and How?

    increases by 1 %，the revenue of luxury good markets will increase by 2.14% million dollars ($ 21,400).

  •   Urban Population
    increases by 1 %，the revenue of luxury good markets will increase by 0.97% million dollars ($ 9,700).

  •        Corruption
       Perception Score
    decreases by 1%，the revenue of luxury goods markets will decrease by 0.35% million dollars ($ 3,500).

  •     Education Index
    has no significant influence on the revenue of luxury good markets.


  • International Arrivals
    increases by 1%，the revenue of luxury goods markets will decrease by 0.11% million dollars ($1,100 dollars).
