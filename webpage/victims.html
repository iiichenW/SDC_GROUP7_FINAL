Key data on wildlife trade

This diagram shows the difference of concentration of importing wildlife products by continents, which seems like have a similar ranking all these years.
Africa and North America have the first and second most frequencies all the time, but the gap between them changes each year. On the contrary, South America always has the least frequencies.
Furthermore, we could figure out the amount of importing trades slightly fluctuated down yearly.

This diagram shows the difference of concentration of exporting wildlife products by continents, which seems like have a similar ranking all these years.
Africa in the picture stayed on top consistently, which reached the peak of 7192 in 2013, while Asia was on the opposite.
Furthermore, we could figure out the amount of exporting trades slightly fluctuated down yearly.

This diagram shows the difference of concentration of the origins of wildlife products by continents, which seems like have a similar ranking all these years.
North America stayed on top consistently, which reached the peak of 6166 in 2013, while Europe was on the opposite.
Furthermore, we could figure out the amount of exporting trades fluctuated down yearly.

From this graph, we can see W and C are major sources. W means Specimens taken from the wild. Products from W slowly went down from 7000 to 5000 during this period. C means Animals bred in captivity in accordance with Resolution Conf. 10.16 (Rev.), as well as parts and derivatives thereof, exported under the provisions of Article VII, paragraph 5, of the Convention. Products from W slightly went down from 5000 to 4500 during this period. Other sources overall stay stable below 1200.

It can be clearly learnt that leather products (large), leather product(small) and skins are the main terms of wildlife transaction. This could clearly show the Inseparable relationship between luxury industry and wildlife trade. Historically, the proportion didn’t change a lot.
Leather products (large) account for roughly 4~7%, skins is less than 18% and leather product(small) is over 75%.

The most common animals in the trade


Python Reticulatus

The reticulated python is a snake species in the family Pythonidae native to South and Southeast Asia. It is the world's longest snake and listed as least concern on the IUCN Red List because of its wide distribution. In several range countries, it is hunted for its skin, for use in traditional medicine, and for sale as a pet.

Crocodylus Niloticus

The Crocodylus niloticus is a large crocodilian native to freshwater habitats in Africa. It is widely distributed throughout sub-Saharan Africa, occurring mostly in the central, eastern, and southern regions of the continent, and lives in different types of aquatic environments such as lakes, rivers, swamps, and marshlands.

Bobcat

The bobcat is a medium-sized North American cat that first appeared during the Irvingtonian stage around 1.8 million years ago (AEO). Although bobcats have been hunted extensively by humans, both for sport and fur, their population has proven resilient though declining in some areas.

Loxodonta Africana

The African bush elephant, also known as the African savanna elephant, is the largest living terrestrial animal. Since 2004, it has been listed as Vulnerable on the IUCN Red List. It is threatened foremost by habitat destruction, and in parts of its range also by poaching for meat and ivory.

Hippopotamus Amphibious

The hippopotamus is a large, mostly herbivorous, semiaquatic mammal and ungulate native to sub-Saharan Africa. Hippos inhabit rivers, lakes, and mangrove swamps. Their reproduction and childbirth both occur in water.

Ursus americanus

American black bear is a medium-sized bear. It is the continent's smallest and most widely distributed bear species. The American black bear is the world's most common bear species. Along with the brown bear, it is one of only two of the eight modern bear species not considered by the IUCN to be globally threatened with extinction.

Equus zebra Hartmannae

The Hartmann's mountain zebra (Equus zebra hartmannae) is a subspecies of the mountain zebra found in far south-western Angola and western Namibia. Hartmann's mountain zebras prefer to live in small groups of 7-12 individuals. They are agile climbers and are able to live in arid conditions and steep mountainous country.

Panthera Tigris

The tiger is the largest extant cat species and a member of the genus Panthera. It is most recognisable for its dark vertical stripes on orange-brown fur with a lighter underside. Many people in China and other parts of Asia have a belief that various tiger parts have medicinal properties, including as pain killers and aphrodisiacs.

  • About Group 'Industry 4.0'

    Our group is made of 4 students from CASA, UCL. We have the common concern on the wildlife protection. We try to explore the Inseparable relationship between luxury industry and wildlife trade through the visualization of data from CITES and the driving force behind luxury industry.

  • About Website

    This website is an assignment of our course——Spatial Data Capture Storage and Analysis. Apart from the purpose of learning website building skills, this website intends to analyze the wildlife trade from the dataset of CITES in the period of 2012-2018.
    Through presenting the truth we hope to reveal the Inseparable relationship between luxury industry and wildlife trade， then recall people’s awareness of protecting wildlife by consuming less luxury goods made from wild animals.
