Luxury Industry
In economics, a luxury good (or upmarket good) is a good for which demand increases more than proportionally as income rises, so that expenditures on the good become a greater proportion of overall spending. (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luxury_goods) Historically, Wild animal products were wildly used in the producing of luxury goods. The scarcity of wildlife caused the high value and iconic meaning of showing high social status in many cultures. However, the awareness of animal protection has raised yearly. More and more brands begin to say NO to wild animal products such as furs.

Wildlife Trade
All over the world, wild animals are suffering painfully because of the animal products trade. The trade in endangered animals whether elephant ivory, rhino horn or animals captured as exotic pets – is a growing threat pushing thousands of species to the brink of extinction. As a student and a consumer, we noticed that the connection between wildlife products consuming and luxury industry. The skins, feather, furs using in the glamorous coats, jackets, shoes, were all once part of animal bodies. The cruelty behind these is entirely about greed.

  • About Group 'Industry 4.0'

    Our group is made of 4 students from CASA, UCL. We have the common concern on the wildlife protection. We try to explore the Inseparable relationship between luxury industry and wildlife trade through the visualization of data from CITES and the driving force behind luxury industry.

  • About Website

    This website is an assignment of our course——Spatial Data Capture Storage and Analysis. Apart from the purpose of learning website building skills, this website intends to analyze the wildlife trade from the dataset of CITES in the period of 2012-2018.
    Through presenting the truth we hope to reveal the Inseparable relationship between luxury industry and wildlife trade， then recall people’s awareness of protecting wildlife by consuming less luxury goods made from wild animals.
